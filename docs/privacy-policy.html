ParkSave

Privacy Policy

Last updated: 31 May 2026

Short version: ParkSave stores everything locally on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or sell any personal data. No account is required.

1. Who we are

ParkSave is an Android application developed by airm_studio. If you have any questions about this policy, contact us at projectai.rm@gmail.com.

2. What data the app uses

ParkSave requests the following device permissions:

  • Location (GPS) — used to record where your car is parked and to show your current position while walking back. Location data is stored only on your device and is never sent to any server.
  • Camera — optionally used to take a photo of your parking spot (floor sign, row number, surroundings). Photos are stored locally in the app and are never uploaded.
  • Notifications — used to send a local reminder 10 minutes before your parking timer expires. Notifications are scheduled entirely on-device; no push notification service is involved.
  • Vibration — used for haptic feedback when you arrive at your parked car.

3. How data is stored

All parking spots, notes, photos, and timer settings are stored in your device's local storage (localStorage / app sandbox). This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly use the Share button, which invokes your device's native share sheet under your control.

Uninstalling the app permanently deletes all stored data.

4. Third-party services

When you view a map or request a walking route, the app loads map tiles and routing data from OpenStreetMap and OSRM (open-source routing). These requests include your approximate location (to load the correct tiles) but do not include any identifying information. Please refer to the OpenStreetMap Foundation Privacy Policy for details.

When you tap Open in Google Maps, the app opens the Google Maps application or website. Google's own privacy policy applies at that point.

5. Data sharing

We do not share, sell, rent, or trade any data with third parties. There are no analytics SDKs, advertising SDKs, or crash-reporting services embedded in ParkSave.

6. Children's privacy

ParkSave is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

7. Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, the new version will be published at this URL with an updated date at the top. Continued use of the app after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
